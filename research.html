Syntactic Retrieval Cues

My thesis work examines the role of specifier positions as cues in a content-addressable retrieval mechanism leveraged during the resolution of long distance subject-verb dependencies.

Related work explores the Subject-Object Asymmetry in preposition-embedded questions, and provides evidence that canonical animacy/thematic role alignment configurations guide dependency processing more generally. slides

Game of Tones

In collaboration with members of the Zapotec Language Project, I developed an online memory game intended to teach participants about lexical tones in Santiago Laxopa Zapotec. This work was developed into a workshop on field methods and digital games for SSILA in January, 2023. More information and resources pertaining to the workshop can be found here.

Contextual Animacy

As a Research Assistant with r/lab, we investigated the contextual sensitivity of animacy in relative clause processing using the Maze task. We presented our findings at the 35th Conference on Human Sentence Processing. slides

Implicit Prosody

As an undergraduate research assistant for Nick Van Handel, we explored the assignment of implicit prosody during silent reading using delexicalized melodies in self-paced reading and Maze tasks.
