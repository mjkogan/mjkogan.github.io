I'm a second-year Master's student in the Department of Linguistics at UC Santa Cruz, where I am advised by Matt Wagers.

My primary research interests are in sentence processing and syntax, with an emphasis on studying the structural representations that guide online parsing operations. I am currently working on my thesis, exploring the nature and granularity of syntactic cues that are leveraged during the resolution of long-distance dependencies.

I am a co-organizer (with Delaney Gomez-Jackson) of Nido de Lenguas, an organization dedicated to the celebration and revitalization of indigenous Oaxacan languages, and a co-coordinator (with Eli Sharf) of SPLAP, the Semantics, Pragmatics and LAnguage Philosophy reading group.
